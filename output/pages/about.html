About

I'm a PhD candidate in Chemical Engineering working with Professor Sharon Glotzer at the University of Michigan. I'm an NSF Graduate Research Fellow, Point Scholar, and recipient of an ACM/SIGHPC Intel Computational & Data Science Fellowship. I'm lucky that this support has allowed me intellectual freedom in grad school most PhD students don't get. Currently, I use computational methods to study how materials self-assemble in non-equilibrium conditions.

Previously, I was a Consultant at the Boston Consulting Group and did my B.S. in Chemical Engineering with a minor in Writing from MIT.
